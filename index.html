Common Cyber Frauds

Recognize Scams Before They Happen

Understanding common online frauds is the first step towards protecting yourself and your loved ones.

UPI Fraud

Fraudsters trick users into approving fake payment requests or sharing confidential UPI PINs.

  • Never share your UPI PIN.
  • Always verify payment requests.
  • Use only trusted payment apps.

Phishing

Fake emails, SMS messages and websites attempt to steal passwords and banking details.

  • Check website URLs carefully.
  • Avoid clicking unknown links.
  • Enable two-factor authentication.

OTP Scam

Scammers pretend to be bank officials and ask for OTP to steal money.

  • Never share OTP with anyone.
  • Banks never ask for OTP.
  • Report suspicious calls immediately.

Fake Job Offers

Scammers promise high-paying jobs and demand registration or processing fees.

  • Never pay to get a job.
  • Verify the employer.
  • Research company reviews.

Lottery Scam

Messages claim you've won a lottery and ask for fees or bank details.

  • Ignore unexpected prize messages.
  • Never pay to claim prizes.
  • Verify with official sources.

Social Media Scam

Fake accounts and fraudulent advertisements target users through social platforms.

  • Verify profiles before trusting.
  • Avoid unknown investment links.
  • Report fake accounts.
Cyber Safety Tips

Stay One Step Ahead of Cyber Criminals

Simple precautions can protect your money, identity and personal information.

Use Strong Passwords

Create unique passwords with uppercase, lowercase, numbers and special characters. Never reuse passwords.

Enable Two-Factor Authentication

Protect your online accounts using OTP or authentication apps wherever available.

Verify Emails

Never click suspicious links received through unknown emails or SMS.

Secure Banking

Use only official banking websites and apps. Avoid public Wi-Fi for financial transactions.

Verify Before Paying

Double-check QR codes, UPI IDs and payment requests before approving transactions.

Report Cyber Crime

Immediately report cyber fraud by calling the National Cyber Crime Helpline (1930).
